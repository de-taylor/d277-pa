About Billings

Billings, Montana has the highest population in the state. It has played a role as the trade capital of Montana since it was founded in 1882 as a railroad settlement. Billings was named after Northern Pacific Railway president Frederick H. Billings.

Billings is known as the "Magic City", since like magic, it appeared overnight in 1882, growing to more than 2,000 residents in just a few months as the railroad town took shape.

Billings is contained in the Yellowstone Valley, a canyon carved by the Yellowstone River. It boasts a reputation as a major milestone on Lewis and Clark's expedition after the Louisiana purchase. On July 25th, 1806, around 4:00 PM, the expedition passed through what would become Billings, stopping at Pompey's Pillar. William Clark carved his name and the date into the pillar, which is now known as the Pompeys Pillar National Monument, and marked as an Area of Environmental Concern by the Bureau of Land Management due to the high tourism traffic the monument sees every year.

This area is home to the Crow Nation, who currently reside just southeast of Billings.
