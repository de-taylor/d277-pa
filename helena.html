About Helena

As the capital of Montana, Helena is known as the "Queen City of the Rockies". It was established first as a gold camp during the Montana gold rush in the 1850's, and by 1888 had approximately 50 millionaires. Helena was made the capital of the Montana territory in 1875, and when Montana was incorporated into the United States in 1889, it was kept as the state Capital.

Helena was a famous stop on Lewis and Clark's expedition after the Louisiana purchase, and the culture of the city revolves around their journey.

Helena's official symbol is "The Guardian of the Gulch", seen in their official logo. This was a wooden fire tower built in the 1870s originally, and rebuilt in 1886. It stood on Tower Hill, overlooking downtown Helena until August 2nd, 2016 when it was damanged by arson and declared structurally unsound. There are currently plans to rebuild "The Guardian" using original construction methods as a monument to its service.
