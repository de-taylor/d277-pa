Welcome to Montana, Big Sky Country

Crisp air, clear water, and breathtaking views are the norm in Montana. Known for its opportunities for outdoor recreation, this state offers a diverse environment to explore.

Montana is divided into two major sections by the Continental Divide, with its eastern half boasting expansive western prairie and badlands, and its western half home to the northern Rocky Mountains.

Recent State History

Montana initially came under American Sovereignty through the Louisiana Purchase from France in 1803, and was explored by the Lewis and Clark Expedition soon afterward. Until gold was discovered in 1852, the Montana Territory was primarily home to several Native American Tribes, as well as the fur trappers who represented most of the American economic activity in the state.

The gold rush brought massive settlement to the state, along with the 1862 Homestead Acts. Major settlements were established around Helena, Billings, and Butte, and mining provided the main economic driver until the mid-20th century.

Today, Montana's largest growing industry is tourism, with an estimated 12.6 million tourists visiting each year. Its nicknames underline its reputation, boasting to be "Big Sky Country", "The Treasure State", and "The Last Best Place". No matter why you're there, it's always a great time to explore Montana.
