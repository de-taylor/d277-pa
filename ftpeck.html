About Fort Peck

Fort Peck was originally established as a trading post by its namesake Colonel Campell K. Peck and his partner Commander E.H. Durfee in 1867. Its original purpose was to facilitate the fur trade with the Assiniboine and Sioux tribes to the north, but over time it gained a post office and other amenities.

In 1934, the U.S. Army established Fort Peck as a "temporary" administrative town in order for the Army Corps of Engineers to oversee the completion of the Fort Peck Reservoir and Dam started in 1933. Today, the town is a permanent settlment, albeit a very small one, whose main employer is still the Army Corps of Engineers.

Rather than remaining purely utilitarian, the town has gained a reputation as a popular recreation spot, as locals and visitors flock to Fort Peck lake for boating, kayaking, fishing, hiking, camping, and cooking outdoors.

To learn more about the history of the area, visitors are welcome at the Fort Peck Interpretive Center, and can even visit the lobby of the power plant at the Dam to learn more about its construction and history.
